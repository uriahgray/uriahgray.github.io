A collection of readers exploring working, thinking and being online. These websites were made by third-year graphic design students of Monash University in first semester of 2017. This class was led by Uriah Gray. (More..)

Context

Digital technology is no longer a revolutionary phenomenon but a normal part of everyday life. Today with countless devices, apps and websites that are designed to filter, simplify, push and aggregate published content, we now read electronically not physically. We play our text through our earphones, read the news headlines through quick-view cards, and we endlessly scroll through timelines.

These large online institutions shape the media being created on them, making some choices seem natural and easy, while hiding other possibilities. We need to challenge the homogenisation of digital content and reclaim this space by being subversive and experimental.

The mutation of music and film into bits and bytes, downloads and streams is now taken for granted...for the world of publishing however, this transformation has only just begun. (Alejandro Ludovico's PostDigital Print)
